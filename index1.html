What is a Supervolcano?

A volcano is an opening in the Earth's crust where lava, volcanic ash, and gases trapped within the Earth are able to escape. Usually when you see an volcano it would resemble a mountain and at the summit it would have a large opening. Well, when talking about “supervolcanoes” it makes a regular volcano seem like a toy. A “supervolcano” has the power to cause major effects to the world, since it is so much bigger than a regular volcano. To help you better see the difference of size, look at the image provided. This is an image of the supervolcano in yellowstone park. It has a crater area of about 1,500 square miles, compared to a regular volcano which has a shield area of around 700 km or 434 miles.
